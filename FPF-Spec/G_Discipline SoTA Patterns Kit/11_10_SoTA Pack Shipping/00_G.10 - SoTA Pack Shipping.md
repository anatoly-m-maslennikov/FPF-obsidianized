## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:1 - Problem frame ‚Äî Shipping without smuggling semantics

Part G produces many **kit-governed** and **suite-governed** publications or records (harvest packs, CHR/CAL packs, evidence graphs, bridge calibration records, log bundles, parity reports). Without an explicit **pack-boundary governing definition**, ‚Äúshipping‚Äù tends to become:

* an ad‚Äëhoc folder/export ritual (tool‚Äëlocked, not citable), or
* a silent re-specification step (shipping accidentally redefines legality, defaults, or selection semantics), or
* a brittle hand‚Äëoff that cannot support RSCR/refresh (no actionable pins/editions/policies attached).

[[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]] fixes the pack boundary: it defines the **single, normative shipping surface** for Part‚ÄëG outputs ‚Äî **`SoTA‚ÄëPack(Core)`** ‚Äî and a minimal choreography for making shipped artefacts **selector‚Äëready** and **audit‚Äëcitable**, while delegating all Part‚ÄëG‚Äëwide invariants to [[00_G.Core - Part G Core Invariants|G.Core]] (citation/delegation, not restatement).

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:2 - Problem ‚Äî Why naive shipping breaks reuse, legality, and refresh

Naive shipping fails (conceptually) when any of the following occurs:

1. **Format-as-governing-spec.** A concrete export format is treated as ‚Äúthe pack,‚Äù turning a tool choice into a governing pack definition.
2. **Editionless hand‚Äëoffs.** Shipped artefacts omit the edition/policy pins required to replay or compare outcomes, so parity and RSCR become non‚Äëactionable.
3. **Pack smuggles semantics.** Shipping reintroduces ‚Äúconvenience‚Äù rules (hidden scalarisation, competing defaults, private gate decisions), fragmenting the governing spec ref.
4. **Invisible crossings.** Cross-context or cross-plane reuse is present, but the pack does not expose the crossing bundles and penalty policy pins needed for audit and refresh planning.
5. **No method‚Äëof‚Äëobtaining‚Äëoutput disclosure.** Consumers receive outcomes without a minimal, citable trail of *which mechanisms/policies/editions produced them*.
6. **Refresh orphaning.** Telemetry and decay signals exist, but the shipped artefact provides no stable scope keys (`PathId` / `PathSliceId`) and no payload pins for RSCR triggers.

### [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:4.3 - Shipping choreography (normative; governing-definition delegating)

[[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]] prescribes a minimal, governing-definition delegating sequence for composing a shipped pack:

1. **S‚Äë1 ‚Äî Gather & pin.** Collect upstream artefact ids and verify the **required pins** implied by the linkage manifest (edition pins, policy pins, UTS/Path pins).
2. **S‚Äë2 ‚Äî Compose `SoTA‚ÄëPack(Core)` + MOO disclosure.** Assemble the pack object and attach a **`MOOManifest`** that lists the referenced mechanisms/policies/editions that produced the shipped outcomes (ids only; semantics stay with governing definitions).

5. **S‚Äë5 ‚Äî Expose CrossingBundle.** For each GateCrossing relevant to the shipped artefacts, expose the required `CrossingBundle` references (fail fast on missing or non‚Äëconformant bundles when required).
6. **S‚Äë6 ‚Äî Emit telemetry pins for refresh planning.** Whenever illumination increases or archive/OEE pin state changes, emit PathSlice‚Äëkeyed telemetry with policy‚Äëid and the active `‚Ä¶Ref.edition` pins (and QD `EmitterPolicyRef`/`InsertionPolicyRef` when applicable).

### [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:4.7 - Published surfaces must ship kind, source, derivation, lens, and shortlist token

- Published surfaces should carry the selector outcome kind and, when applicable, the set-result kind or handoff kind, plus the subject kind, source set kind, and relevant declared surface pins.
- These are publication payload metadata fields inside `SoTA-Pack(Core)`, not publication face kinds, publication form kinds, interop publication form kinds, or carrier kinds.
- Good publication fields include `selectorOutcomeKind`, `setResultFamily`, `handoffKind`, `subjectKind`, `sourceSetFamily`, `sourceSetComposition`, `dominanceRegime`, `lensId`, `shortlistId`, and any declared archive or promotion-policy ids that the reader needs to interpret the visible set.
- Those payload fields should use controlled tokens, cited ids, or already-declared head labels rather than shipping-local prose values.
- When the visible surface or the shortlisted source is one derived tradition view, also publish the derivation explicitly.
- Useful additional fields there include `derivedViewKind`, `basePaletteRef`, and the declared `qId` or reachability rule id that disciplined that derivation.
- `portfolioMode` may remain as one support field about selector operation, but it should not stand in for the public set label.
- A published surface should mirror semantics that are already declared in the governing palette, front, archive, or shortlist language.
- It should not redefine that semantics locally.
- When one shipped surface still needs a plain-language label, use the declared set-result kind and source set rather than falling back to `portfolioMode`.

- If one shortlist is emitted from that derived tradition front, publish `selectorOutcomeKind=SetResultOutcome`, `setResultFamily=Shortlist`, `sourceSetFamily=Front`, `derivedViewKind=TraditionFront`, `basePaletteRef=SoTAPaletteDescriptionId`, and the named `lensId` together.
- If that same shortlisted surface is emitted as one stable public object, also publish `shortlistId=<...>` and keep it recoverable that the token names that shortlist rather than replacing it.
- If one retained tradition archive view is shown, publish `selectorOutcomeKind=SetResultOutcome`, `setResultFamily=Archive`, `sourceSetFamily=Archive`, `derivedViewKind=TraditionArchive`, and keep the same `basePaletteRef` recoverable.
- If the shortlist is later ordered, publish `setResultFamily=RankedShortlist` and keep the declared source set visible.
- Do not publish `setResultFamily=ChoiceSet` unless the shipped object is explicitly one mathematical analysis artifact rather than the public selected set.
- Do not publish `sourceSetFamily=TraditionPalette` alone when the visible object is already one derived tradition view; readers need to know which view is on the surface and which base palette it depends on.
- Do not publish `TraditionFront` or `TraditionArchive` as if they were the default meaning of `Tradition`.
- Do not ask `portfolioMode` to tell the reader whether they are seeing one palette, one front, one archive, or one shortlist.

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:5 - Consequences

**Benefits**

* A shipped result becomes **selector‚Äëready** and **audit‚Äëcitable** without turning file formats into governing specifications.
* Shipping is no longer a semantic ‚Äúbackdoor‚Äù: pack‚Äëlevel semantics remain governing-definition delegated.
* RSCR/refresh becomes operationally viable because pack‚Äëlevel scope keys and payload pins are present.

**Costs / trade‚Äëoffs**

* Shipping becomes more explicit (more pins and explicit surfaces), which raises authoring overhead.
* If upstream governing definitions fail to provide citable ids/pins, [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]] cannot paper over the gap; shipping will block or ship a visibly incomplete pack (depending on policy‚Äëbound failure behaviour, governed by cited definitions).

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:6 - Bias‚ÄëAnnotation (informative)

Lenses tested: **Gov**, **Arch**, **Onto/Epist**, **Prag**, **Did**.

* **Format bias (Arch/Prag).** A popular export format is tempting to treat as ‚Äúthe pack‚Äù.
  *Mitigation:* keep Core surfaces conceptual ([[02_E.05.02 - Notational Independence|E.5.2]]); move serialisation recipes to Annex/Interop; keep conformance on semantics.
* **Centralisation bias (Gov).** A single pattern governing shipping semantics can become a bottleneck.
  *Mitigation:* keep shipping as one explicit governing-pattern responsibility, but push mode/method specifics into explicit `G.10:Ext.*` extension blocks and cite governing patterns.
* **Telemetry‚Üídominance bias (Onto/Prag).** Shipping pipelines often ‚Äúpromote‚Äù telemetry proxies (illumination/coverage) into ranking.
  *Mitigation:* preserve the telemetry/order separation and require explicit CAL policy‚Äëid for any promotion; record the policy‚Äëid in audit pins/telemetry.
* **Interop authority bias (Onto/Epist).** External indexes can silently override local legality/typing.
  *Mitigation:* `G.10‚Äë6` ingests interop only as cited notes (editions + mapping policy refs), never as a replacement governing spec ref.

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:7 - Archetypal grounding (informative; post‚Äë2015 method families)

**World‚Äëplane (benchmark shipping).**
A CG‚ÄëFrame ships a selected set that includes a QD archive (e.g., MAP‚ÄëElites‚Äëclass / CMA‚ÄëME‚Äëclass families) and a generator family (e.g., POET‚Äëclass environment generation). The shipped `SoTA‚ÄëPack(Core)` cites the CHR/CAL packs and records the QD/OEE extension-required pins through the extension blocks so that downstream parity and refresh can be scoped to the affected `PathSliceId`s rather than forcing a global rebuild.

**Episteme‚Äëplane (synthesis shipping).**
A CG‚ÄëFrame ships a pluralistic set of admissible methods gathered from post‚Äë2015 literature streams (living review + synthesis pack). The shipped pack carries explicit CN/CG spec refs, evidence path citations, and method‚Äëof‚Äëobtaining‚Äëoutput disclosure; downstream selection uses set‚Äëvalued outcomes and can schedule refresh when the synthesis pack or key pins change.

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:8.1 - Anti‚Äëpatterns and remedies

* **AP‚Äë1 Format‚Äëas‚Äëgoverning‚Äëspecification.** Remedy: keep Core surfaces conceptual ([[02_E.05.02 - Notational Independence|E.5.2]]); move serialisation to Annex/Interop; enforce `CC‚ÄëG10.1`.
* **AP‚Äë2 Hidden edition drift.** Remedy: require `‚Ä¶Ref.edition` pins in AuditPins and treat edition changes as RSCR‚Äërelevant via canonical trigger kinds.
* **AP‚Äë3 ‚ÄúQD archive present‚Äù but missing definition pins.** Remedy: enforce `CC‚ÄëG10.2` and the `G.10:Ext.QDArchiveShippingPins` pin declarations.
* **AP‚Äë4 Telemetry silently becomes dominance.** Remedy: keep telemetry report‚Äëonly unless an explicit CAL policy promotes it; require policy‚Äëid recorded (ties to `CC‚ÄëG10.3` and MOO discipline).
* **AP‚Äë5 No PathSlice key ‚Üí refresh becomes global.** Remedy: enforce PathSlice‚Äëkeyed telemetry and path citations (`G.10‚Äë4`, `G.10‚Äë5`).
* **AP‚Äë6 Cross‚ÄëContext reuse without visible crossing pins.** Remedy: require `CrossingBundleIds` + Bridge/CL policy pins; fail fast on missing/non‚Äëconformant bundles (`CC‚ÄëG10.7`).
* **AP‚Äë7 Interop ingestion rewrites semantics.** Remedy: ingest interop as cited notes only; semantics remain in [[00_G.13 - External Interop Hooks for SoTA Discipline Packs (conceptual)|G.13]] (`G.10‚Äë6`, `G.10:Ext.InteropCitation`).
* **AP‚Äë8 Derived-view collapse.** Remedy: ship `sourceSetFamily`, `derivedViewKind`, `basePaletteRef`, and the declared `Q` or reachability basis with enough explicitness that one derived tradition view cannot masquerade as the default palette meaning.

## [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]:8.2 - SoTA‚ÄëEchoing (post‚Äë2015, for orientation)

* **Research‚Äëobject packaging & provenance.** Post‚Äë2015 practice increasingly treats ‚Äúrelease artefacts‚Äù as *packages with explicit provenance, versions, and minimal replay pins* (e.g., modern research‚Äëobject and RO‚ÄëCrate‚Äëclass approaches). [[00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]] mirrors the ‚Äúpackage‚Äëas‚Äëcitation‚Äësurface‚Äù idea while keeping semantics governing-definition delegated.
* **Reproducibility regimes in ML/AI.** Contemporary reproducibility checklists, artifact evaluation/badging, and benchmark reporting norms motivate: explicit version pins, explicit method disclosure, and separating telemetry summaries from decision criteria unless policy‚Äëpromoted.
* **Scholarly KG interoperability.** ORKG/OpenAlex‚Äëclass ecosystems highlight the need to treat external mappings as *interop notes with editions*, not as replacement governing spec refs ‚Äî matching the `G.10‚Äë6` and `G.10:Ext.InteropCitation` stance.
