## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:1 - Problem frame

You are authoring a **CG‚ÄĎFrame** and want a **repeatable scaffold** that connects:

* a declared **scope anchor** (`CG‚ÄĎFrameContext`, `entityOfConcern`, governing spec refs),
* a **local SoTA set** (scoped and provenance‚ÄĎanchored),
* a **variant pool** (candidate ideas / decision options / method variants),
* a **shortlist** (a set-result outcome, not a forced singleton),
* **publication‚ÄĎready bindings** into Part‚ÄĎF artefacts (UTS rows, Name Cards, RSCR tests, worked examples),
* and **refresh readiness** (telemetry hooks + RSCR wiring) without redefining refresh or shipping.

This pattern is intentionally **a chassis**, not a method specification:

* harvesting semantics are governed by [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/03_02_SoTA Harvester & Synthesis/00_G.02 - SoTA Harvester & Synthesis|G.2]],
* selection/dispatch semantics are governed by [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/06_05_Multi-Method Dispatcher and MethodFamily Registry/00_G.05 - Multi-Method Dispatcher and MethodFamily Registry|G.5]],
* CHR/CAL payload semantics are governed by [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/04_03_CHR Authoring for a CG-Frame- Characteristics, Scales, Levels, Coordinates/00_G.03 - CHR Authoring for a CG-Frame- Characteristics, Scales, Levels, Coordinates|G.3]] / [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/05_04_CAL Authoring for a CG-Frame- Operators, Acceptance Clauses, Evidence Wiring/00_G.04 - CAL Authoring for a CG-Frame- Operators, Acceptance Clauses, Evidence Wiring|G.4]],

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:2 - Problem

Without a chassis, CG‚ÄĎFrame authoring tends to fail in repeatable ways:

* **SoTA is not locally scoped**: inputs are ‚Äúin the air‚ÄĚ, not a reconstructible set.
* **Generation is ad‚ÄĎhoc**: variant candidates are emitted without a stable trace of why/when/how.
* **Selection is opaque**: eligibility/acceptance and assurance are not pinned to explicit surfaces.
* **Outputs don‚Äôt land in reusable surfaces**: no clean hand‚ÄĎoff into UTS / RoleDescription / Concept‚ÄĎSets / RSCR.
* **No kit‚ÄĎlevel snapshot**: the scaffold lacks a versioned manifest, so downstream can‚Äôt reliably cite ‚Äúwhich chassis edition‚ÄĚ was used.
* **Refresh is unplanned**: there is no canonical wiring from edits/telemetry/decay to RSCR causes along the P2W path.

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:3 - Forces

* **Breadth vs. precision:** harvest wide enough to avoid local dogma, but keep the artefact actionable.
* **Generativity vs. assurance:** encourage novelty while keeping evidence, legality, and trust inspectable.
* **Local meaning vs. portability:** keep meaning local by default; crossing must be explicit and auditable.
* **Expressiveness vs. parsimony:** resist inventing new types/slots; prefer reuse and explicit wiring.
* **Stability vs. evolution:** keep stable IDs and pins while allowing SoTA, policies, and editions to evolve.
* **Didactic clarity vs. normative minimalism:** authors need a concrete scaffold, but universal invariants must not be duplicated outside [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/00_Core_Part G Core Invariants/00_G.Core - Part G Core Invariants|G.Core]].

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:5 - Archetypal Grounding ‚ÄĒ Tell‚ÄďShow‚ÄďShow (informative)

**Tell.** Use the six‚ÄĎcard chassis to make a CG‚ÄĎFrame authoring effort reproducible: a scoped SoTA set, a traceable candidate pool, a set‚ÄĎreturn shortlist, a publishable library index, and refresh readiness‚ÄĒwithout redefining spec-legality/selection/refresh governing definitions.

**Show A (R&D multi‚ÄĎcriteria decisions; post‚ÄĎ2015 SoTA practice).**

* **M1:** define `CG‚ÄĎFrameContext` for ‚ÄúR&D decision options‚ÄĚ, pin `CNSpecRef/CGSpecRef` editions, and publish `entityOfConcern` + `ReferencePlane`.
* **M2:** build `SoTA_SetId` via [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/03_02_SoTA Harvester & Synthesis/00_G.02 - SoTA Harvester & Synthesis|G.2]] using a living‚ÄĎreview style funnel (e.g., PRISMA‚ÄĎlike trace + update cadence) and publish UTS stubs for reusable constructs.
* **M3:** emit a `VariantPoolId` where each candidate cites its emitter policy and provenance; if QD is used, wire `DescriptorMapRef.edition` and `DistanceDefRef.edition` via `G.1:Ext.NQD`.
* **M4:** produce `ShortlistId` as a selected-set / shortlist surface via [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/06_05_Multi-Method Dispatcher and MethodFamily Registry/00_G.05 - Multi-Method Dispatcher and MethodFamily Registry|G.5]], with acceptance predicates sourced from [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/05_04_CAL Authoring for a CG-Frame- Operators, Acceptance Clauses, Evidence Wiring/00_G.04 - CAL Authoring for a CG-Frame- Operators, Acceptance Clauses, Evidence Wiring|G.4]].
* **M5:** publish a `CGFrameLibraryId` indexing the chosen CHR/CAL/LOG bundles and UTS rows; register RSCR tests.
* **M6:** declare refresh readiness (telemetry pins + canonical RSCR trigger kinds) and wire to [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/12_11_Telemetry-Driven Refresh and Decay Orchestrator/00_G.11 - Telemetry-Driven Refresh and Decay Orchestrator|G.11]].

**Show B (clinical operations; safety‚ÄĎfirst acceptability).**

* **M1:** scope a CG‚ÄĎFrame around dose adjustment decisions; pin legality and evidence minima explicitly.
* **M2:** harvest SoTA models and safety constraints as a reconstructible set (governed by [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/03_02_SoTA Harvester & Synthesis/00_G.02 - SoTA Harvester & Synthesis|G.2]]).
* **M3:** generate policy‚ÄĎconstrained candidate protocols; emitter trace and evidence pins are mandatory.
* **M4:** shortlist remains a set; ‚Äúchoose one‚ÄĚ remains an explicit policy decision, not silently baked into the generator.
* **M5/M6:** publish and wire refresh (decay events, policy changes, and evidence updates retrigger along the P2W path).

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:6 - Bias‚ÄĎAnnotation (informative)

* **Recency bias:** ‚Äúnewest paper wins‚ÄĚ (mitigate with explicit inclusion criteria and update cadence in [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/03_02_SoTA Harvester & Synthesis/00_G.02 - SoTA Harvester & Synthesis|G.2]] wiring).
* **Novelty bias:** over‚ÄĎrewarding novelty at the expense of legality/assurance (mitigate by making acceptance and assurance pins explicit and governed).
* **Algorithmic favoritism:** baking a preferred generator into ‚Äúthe chassis‚ÄĚ (mitigate by keeping M3 method‚ÄĎagnostic and pushing methods into Extensions).
* **Scalarisation bias:** collapsing selected sets or partial orders into a single score (mitigate by set‚ÄĎreturn discipline pinned through [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/00_Core_Part G Core Invariants/00_G.Core - Part G Core Invariants|G.Core]]).
* **Hidden‚ÄĎcrossing bias:** implicit reuse across contexts (mitigate by explicit crossing pins and Bridge‚ÄĎonly routing via [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/00_Core_Part G Core Invariants/00_G.Core - Part G Core Invariants|G.Core]]).

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:8 - Common Anti‚ÄĎPatterns and How to Avoid Them (informative)

* **Anti‚ÄĎpattern: ‚ÄúShadow CN/CG spec inside the chassis.‚ÄĚ**
  *Avoid:* keep CN/CG as cited governing spec refs; use pins and governing definition references only.

* **Anti‚ÄĎpattern: ‚ÄúChassis hard‚ÄĎcodes a favourite algorithm.‚ÄĚ**
  *Avoid:* keep M3 core method‚ÄĎagnostic; add algorithm families only via Extensions with explicit governing patterns and edition pins.

* **Anti‚ÄĎpattern: ‚ÄúShortlist = one winner.‚ÄĚ**
  *Avoid:* preserve selected-set returns; any singleton choice must be an explicit downstream decision rule (policy‚ÄĎbound).

* **Anti‚ÄĎpattern: ‚ÄúRefresh plan described as prose triggers.‚ÄĚ**
  *Avoid:* record canonical `RSCRTriggerKindId` and payload pins; aliases only as labels and only if docked.

* **Anti-pattern: ‚ÄúPackaging implies shipping governance.‚ÄĚ**
  *Avoid:* treat M5 as a library index; treat M6 as readiness wiring; ship only via [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/11_10_SoTA Pack Shipping/00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]].

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:9 - Consequences (informative)

* **Repeatable authoring:** CG‚ÄĎFrame work becomes reconstructible: what exists, what it depends on, and how it is refreshed.
* **Method pluralism with discipline:** multiple generator/selector families can coexist without turning the chassis into a shadow method spec.
* **Better reuse:** outputs land directly in published artefacts (UTS/Name/RSCR‚ÄĎready) rather than remaining local notes.
* **Lower refactor cost:** method changes localise to Extensions; core invariants remain stable and one governing definition.

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:10 - Rationale (informative)

* **Why six cards?** It matches the minimal decomposition needed to keep scope, harvesting, generation, selection, publication, and refresh **explicitly separable** (and thus auditable and evolvable).
* **Why ‚Äúkit/index‚ÄĚ rather than ‚Äúpack‚ÄĚ?** A CG‚ÄĎFrame authoring effort must stay modular; shipping is a separate governing boundary ([[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/11_10_SoTA Pack Shipping/00_G.10 - SoTA Pack Shipping|G.10]]).
* **Why push method content into Extensions?** It prevents conflating (i) universal invariants, (ii) frame‚ÄĎspecific kit surfaces, and (iii) method/generator families‚ÄĒsupporting Phase‚ÄĎ2 universalisation goals.
* **Why working‚ÄĎmodel first?** Many CG‚ÄĎFrames fail due to premature formalism; a chassis with didactic micro‚ÄĎexamples improves correctness of pins, names, and boundaries before deep formalisation.

## [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/02_01_CG-Frame-Ready Generator/00_G.01 - CG-Frame-Ready Generator|G.1]]:11 - SoTA‚ÄĎEchoing (informative)

This chassis is designed to stay compatible with modern (post‚ÄĎ2015) practice without confusing ‚ÄúSoTA‚ÄĚ with ‚Äúcurrently popular‚ÄĚ:

* **Evidence synthesis:** living systematic review protocols (e.g., PRISMA‚ÄĎstyle traceability and update cadence) map naturally to M2 wiring governed by [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/03_02_SoTA Harvester & Synthesis/00_G.02 - SoTA Harvester & Synthesis|G.2]].

* **Open‚ÄĎended exploration:** post‚ÄĎ2015 open‚ÄĎendedness systems (POET‚ÄĎclass, paired/adversarial environment generation lines, and modern curriculum‚ÄĎgeneration approaches) fit when treated as generator‚ÄĎfamily wiring (governed elsewhere) rather than as chassis semantics.
* **Set‚ÄĎvalued decision outputs:** modern multi‚ÄĎobjective and set‚ÄĎvalued evaluation practices align with the [[G_Discipline SoTA Patterns Kit/00_Core_Part G Core Invariants/00_G.Core - Part G Core Invariants|G.Core]] set‚ÄĎreturn discipline, preventing hidden scalarisation.
* **Governed traceability:** contemporary reproducibility and accountability norms (mechanism disclosure, provenance anchors, and audit trails) are supported via pinned policies/editions and explicit module boundaries, without introducing data‚ÄĎgovernance machinery.
