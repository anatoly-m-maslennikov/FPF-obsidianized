> **Type:** D-family ethical mediation and decision-use pattern
> **Status:** Stable
> **Pattern role:** This compact pattern owns the ethical use of an already mapped conflict: mediation, refusal, evidence demand, bounded decision use, and residual handling.

**Use this when.** Use this pattern when an interlevel ethical conflict structure from [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/02_03_Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure/00_D.03 - Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure|D.3]] must be used for mediation, refusal, decision, evidence demand, causal return, assurance return, or architecture return.

**Not this pattern when.** If the conflict structure is not yet mapped, use [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/02_03_Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure/00_D.03 - Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure|D.3]]. If the issue is only value plurality, use [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/00_01_Ethical Value Plurality and FPF Boundary/00_D.01 - Ethical Value Plurality and FPF Boundary|D.1]]. If the issue is only entry recognition, use [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/01_02_Multilevel Ethics For Holon Work/00_D.02 - Multilevel Ethics For Holon Work|D.2]]. If the current work is bias, fairness, impact audit, causal-fairness audit consumption, or ethical assurance, use [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/04_05_Bias Audit and Ethical Assurance/00_D.05 - Bias Audit and Ethical Assurance|D.5]].

**What goes wrong if missed.** A mapped ethical conflict is treated as solved, blocked, or decision-ready without naming mediation, refusal, evidence demand, return, accepted residual, or bounded decision use.

**What this buys.** The practitioner can use a [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/02_03_Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure/00_D.03 - Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure|D.3]] conflict structure for one admissible mediation action or bounded decision use while keeping evidence, causality, assurance, architecture, and bias-audit claims with their owners.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:1 - Problem Frame

Once an interlevel ethical conflict is visible, the next risk is premature closure. A team may declare a compromise before evidence is sufficient, turn an assurance input into ethical permission, use one level's value as a trump card, or hide a refusal behind technical language.

[[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] governs the use of the mapped conflict. It does not make FPF a final moral authority. It asks what move is admissible from the current conflict structure and what must return to evidence, causality, assurance, architecture, decision, or value framing before action is justified.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:1.0 - Problem

A mapped ethical conflict can still be used badly. The failure is to treat a conflict structure, assurance input, formula, or architecture return as if it already selected a compromise, refusal, evidence demand, accepted residual, or bounded decision use.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:1.1 - Forces

| Force | Tension |
| --- | --- |
| Mapped conflict vs. premature closure | A conflict structure makes action discussable, but does not by itself decide compromise, refusal, or permission. |
| Evidence demand vs. decision pressure | Work may need a decision, while the ethical claim still needs stronger evidence, causal analysis, assurance, or architecture return. |
| Mediation vs. universal authority | [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use]] can govern one bounded use of a mapped conflict, but cannot become a general decision theory. |
| Residual acceptance vs. hidden harm | Proceeding under residual harm can be admissible only when residuals, responsibility-bearing role assignments, and return conditions are explicit. |
| Mathematical allocation vs. ethical decision | A formula or optimization can inform a decision, but it is not the ethical decision by itself. |

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:2 - Solution

```text
EthicalMediationDecisionUse@Context:
  conflictStructureRef
  boundedContextRef
  valueFrameRefs
  decisionQuestionRef?
  optionRefs
  proposedMediationRefs?
  refusalOrStopCondition?
  evidenceDemandRefs?
  causalReturnRefs?

  architectureResidualReturnRefs?
  acceptedResidualRefs?
  decisionRecordRefs?
```

The record names the current ethical use of the conflict: mediate, refuse, continue under explicit residual, demand evidence, ask a causal question, ask for assurance, return to architecture, or make a bounded decision.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:4 - Archetypal Grounding (Worked Slices)

**Fair-share case.** A service outage plan can protect hospitals, households, or industrial customers, but not all at once. [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/02_03_Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure/00_D.03 - Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure|D.3]] maps affected scopes and value concerns. [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] records the mediation use: options, accepted residuals, evidence demand, role assignments for decision responsibility, and return conditions. A mathematical allocation method may be cited through [[C_Kernel Extension Specifications/17_29_Mathematical Lens Use/00_C.29 - Mathematical Lens Use|C.29]], but the allocation formula is not the ethical decision by itself.

**Override case.** An assurance review says a release has the required technical assurance relation, but [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/02_03_Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure/00_D.03 - Interlevel Ethical Conflict Structure|D.3]] shows unresolved harm for a subgroup. [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] does not let assurance override the ethical conflict. It records whether release is refused, conditioned, delayed for evidence, returned to [[C_Kernel Extension Specifications/16_28_CausalUse-CAL- Causal-Use Questions, Causality-Ladder Rungs, Identification and Realizability/00_C.28 - CausalUse-CAL- Causal-Use Questions, Causality-Ladder Rungs, Identification and Realizability|C.28]] causal-use analysis, or allowed with explicit residual and responsibility.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:5 - Boundaries

[[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] does not own conflict structure, bias audit, ethical assurance, architecture residual, causal identification, evidence provenance, or decision theory in general. It owns the ethical use of a mapped interlevel conflict.

Do not name a mediation move "calculus" unless a mathematical lens is selected and the lens is actually doing work. Do not name a mediation move "operator" unless the current pattern explicitly governs an operation. Most [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] use is a bounded decision-use record, not a mathematical object.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:7 - Consequences

This pattern makes ethical action reviewable without pretending that every conflict has a clean optimum. It preserves refusal, evidence demand, and residual acceptance as first-class outcomes. It also prevents architecture, assurance, or causal evidence from quietly becoming moral permission.

## [[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]]:9 - Rationale

[[D_Multi-scale Ethics and Conflict Optimization/03_04_Ethical Mediation and Decision Use/00_D.04 - Ethical Mediation and Decision Use|D.4]] exists because an inspectable ethical conflict still needs a bounded use. Some uses stop work. Some demand evidence. Some return to causal, assurance, or architecture owners. Some proceed under an accepted residual with named responsibility and return conditions. Without this pattern, teams either freeze because conflict exists or move too fast because the conflict was mapped once.

The pattern keeps refusal, evidence demand, and residual acceptance visible as ordinary outcomes. It also prevents formulas, assurance labels, architecture residual repairs, or causal claims from silently becoming moral authorization.
