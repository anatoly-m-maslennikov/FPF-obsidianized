## [[05_B.01.05 - őďmethod - Order-Sensitive Method Composition & Work Enactment|B.1.5]]:2 - Problem

Without a dedicated, order‚ÄĎaware method operator:

1. **DesignRunTag conflation.** Authors mix **MethodDescription** (blueprint) and **Work** (execution), producing artifacts that have both planned and executed attributes.
2. **Order erasure.** Sequences with crucial **pre/post‚ÄĎconditions** get collapsed into sets; reordering breaks correctness while still ‚Äúpassing‚ÄĚ naive aggregation.
3. **Capability mismatches.** Step outputs do not match the next step‚Äôs required inputs, but this is hidden in untyped edges; composite methods become non‚ÄĎexecutable.
4. **Work leakage.** Costs and resource flows are **inlined** into method definitions; later models double‚ÄĎcount or violate conservation (őď\_work was created to prevent this).
5. **Synergy by arithmetic.** Throughput or quality jumps caused by **proper joins** or **coordination** are misreported as simple sums or averages‚ÄĒviolating WLNK and obscuring when a **Meta‚ÄĎHolon Transition ([[00_B.02 - Meta-Holon Transition (MHT)- Recognizing Emergence and Re-identifying Wholes|B.2]])** should be declared.

### [[05_B.01.05 - őďmethod - Order-Sensitive Method Composition & Work Enactment|B.1.5]]:4.3 - Core aggregation rules (design‚ÄĎtime composition)

When computing **őď\_method^plan(D\_spec, ŌÉ)**:

1. **Order preservation.**
   Respect the **OrderSpec ŌÉ**; independent branches may be folded in any **topological sort** (őď\_ctx NC‚ÄĎ3). **SerialStepOf** enforces strict precedence; **ParallelFactorOf** allows concurrency with a **join**.

2. **Capability continuity (typed joins).**
   Every join must be **type-sound**: the **post-condition and output signature** of each incoming branch must **meet** the next step's **pre-conditions** (logical entailment or declared **adapter** steps). Missing adapters are **defects**, not assumptions.

3. **MIC synthesis (boundary behaviour).**
   For each external interaction of a step, decide **Promote / Forward / Encapsulate** into the composite **MIC**. This inherits the clarity of BIC ([[02_B.01.02 - System-specific Aggregation őď-sys|B.1.2]]) for methods.

   * *Promote*: becomes a direct composite interaction (e.g., top‚ÄĎlevel ‚Äústart/stop‚ÄĚ).
   * *Forward*: remains step‚ÄĎlocal but exposed under the composite boundary (namespaced).
   * *Encapsulate*: becomes internal; callers cannot rely on it.

4. **Assurance hooks (without computing assurance).**
   Record where **[[00_B.03 - Trust and Assurance Calculus (F-G-R with Congruence)|B.3]] assurance** will later hang: (i) the **cutset** steps that bound reliability/quality, (ii) the **integration edges** whose **CL** will penalise poor fit (mappings, fragile joins), and (iii) the **envelope** (G) intended for the method‚Äôs validity.

5. **No costs here.**
   If a step lists resources/yields, **do not** aggregate them here. Instead, add a pointer to the corresponding **őď\_work** composition to be executed with the same order/joins at run‚ÄĎtime.

### [[05_B.01.05 - őďmethod - Order-Sensitive Method Composition & Work Enactment|B.1.5]]:4.4 - Core aggregation rules (run‚ÄĎtime enactment)

When executing **őď\_method^run(M\_spec, RA, Fill)**:

1. **Role‚ÄďMethod‚ÄďSpec alignment ([[00_A.02 - Role Taxonomy|A.2]] / [[00_A.03 - Transformer Constitution (Quartet)|A.3]] / [[00_A.15 - Role-Method-Work Alignment (Contextual Enactment)|A.15]]).**
   Confirm that `RA.role` is eligible to enact the `U.Method` described by `M_spec` (or a declared equivalent/refinement in the same context), and that the Work‚Äôs `performedBy` and `executedWithin` anchors can be satisfied ([[01_A.15.01 - U.Work|A.15.1]]). If this fails, you may still record an attempted run, but it is **not** a conformant ‚Äúexecution of `M_spec`‚ÄĚ.

2. **Pre/post enforcement.**
   Before each step, verify **pre‚ÄĎconditions** against **Fill** and the evolving carrier state; after, check **post‚ÄĎconditions** hold. Failing these means the run cannot be certified as a conformant `U.Work` execution of `M_spec`.

3. **Typed state flow.**
   The **state/action types** produced by a step must make the next step **well‚ÄĎtyped**; if not, an **adapter method** (itself with a MethodDescription) must be present in the graph.

4. **Order determinism (őď\_ctx).**
   Respect the `OrderSpec ŌÉ` declared in `M_spec`. Parallel branches may execute independently **only if** they share no state that would break **NC‚ÄĎ1..3**; otherwise they must synchronise at the declared join.

5. **MIC honouring.**
   Interactions exposed by **MIC** are the **only** external commitments the composite method makes. Any additional ad‚ÄĎhoc external interaction is a **model violation** (or requires updating the MIC and re‚ÄĎplanning).

6. **őď\_work hand‚ÄĎoff.**
   Invoke **őď\_work** to compute **spent resources, yields, dissipation** along the same order/join structure. The resulting ledgers and work-result records **annotate the Work** but are **not** part of őď\_method‚Äôs aggregation.

> **Invariant intuition.**
>
> * **IDEM:** a single step‚ÄĎmethod composed alone yields the same method.
> * **COMM/LOC:** replaced by őď\_ctx **NC‚ÄĎ1..3** (determinism given `ŌÉ`, context hash of `ŌÉ`, and partial‚ÄĎorder soundness).
> * **WLNK:** quality/throughput of the composite is bounded by the **critical path** steps (identified for later [[00_B.03 - Trust and Assurance Calculus (F-G-R with Congruence)|B.3]] assurance).
> * **MONO:** strengthening a step (better pre/post, stronger type, improved adapter) **cannot** make the composite worse.

### [[05_B.01.05 - őďmethod - Order-Sensitive Method Composition & Work Enactment|B.1.5]]:4.5 - Didactic contrasts (to prevent common confusions)

* **Method vs Work.**
  Method = the semantic ‚Äúway of doing‚ÄĚ (what transformations are admissible); **Work** = what happened this time, including **resources spent / yields / dissipation** when enacting it (őď\_work). Keep them distinct.

* **Method vs Structure.**
  Method composes **ordered steps**; structure composes **parts** (őď\_sys). Do not use **ComponentOf** where **SerialStepOf/ParallelFactorOf** are intended.

* **Step vs part vs specialization.**
  A ‚Äústep‚ÄĚ in `SerialStepOf/ParallelFactorOf` is a **factor in an order algebra**, not a mereological part and not a type‚ÄĎspecialisation.
  ‚Äď Use **ComponentOf/PartOf** for structural wholes ([[00_A.14 - Advanced Mereology- Components, Portions, Aspects & Phases|A.14]]).
  ‚Äď Use **`‚Č§‚āė` refinement / equivalence / substitution** for Method specialisation ([[01_A.03.01 - U.Method- Context-Defined Way of Doing|A.3.1]]).
  ‚Äď Use **Kind‚ÄĎCAL (`‚äĎ`)** for kind/subkind.

* **Method vs Phase.**
  Method composition is **order**; **PhaseOf** (őď\_time) is **temporal progression** of the **same carrier**. If a phase boundary also introduces **closure/supervision/context rebase**, that is **MHT** ([[00_B.02 - Meta-Holon Transition (MHT)- Recognizing Emergence and Re-identifying Wholes|B.2]]), not mere phasing.

* **MethodDescription vs Work.**
  Keep **planning** artefacts (MethodDescription) separate from **run‚ÄĎtime occurrences** (Work). `őď_method^plan` produces MethodDescriptions; `őď_method^run` produces Work that cites an edition‚ÄĎpinned MethodDescription and records effective slot fillings and ledgers ([[01_A.15.01 - U.Work|A.15.1]]).
